Premium Home
Networking

Designed to be displayed. Not hidden.

An industrial design study exploring how premium networking hardware can become part of the home—not something hidden behind it.

Color · Material · Finish
Bamboo

Restrained materials, passive ventilation, and a single ambient status light create a router intended to live comfortably beside books, ceramics, and everyday objects.

Context

Designed to Belong.

Premium networking hardware is often hidden from view. This exploration reimagines the router as a refined object designed to complement modern interiors.

Details

Crafted in Detail.

Material transitions, integrated ventilation, and tactile controls were refined to create a product that feels intentional from every angle.

Diamond‑Knurled Control Dial

A single tactile control creates a quiet, intentional interaction point.

Precision‑Machined Chassis

Soft radiused corners and subtle chamfers give the object a furniture‑grade presence.

Integrated Thermal Venting

Passive airflow is treated as part of the design language rather than a hidden compromise.

Workspace

At Home in the Workspace.

A restrained form language allows the product to complement modern work environments without becoming a visual distraction.

Engineering

Performance With Restraint.

Performance and restraint can coexist. Passive cooling, concealed antennas, and multi‑gig connectivity are integrated within a calm architectural form.
