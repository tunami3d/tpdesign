Restrained materials, passive ventilation, and a single ambient status light create a router intended to live comfortably beside books, ceramics, and everyday objects.

Technology should become quieter as it becomes more capable.

01 — Context

Designed to Belong

Most routers are treated like infrastructure—hidden behind desks, televisions, and furniture. This exploration asks what happens when networking hardware is designed as a considered object within the home.

02 — Process

Form Exploration

Early concepts explored vertical proportions, hidden antennas, integrated thermal paths, and simplified interaction. The goal was a product that feels closer to furniture and premium audio than conventional networking hardware.

03 — Details

Precision in the Details

The detail studies focus on the physical moments that communicate quality: tactility, machining, material transitions, and ventilation that feels integrated rather than applied.

Diamond-Knurled Control Dial

A single tactile control creates an intentional point of interaction.

Precision-Machined Chassis

Soft radii and subtle chamfers reduce visual noise while preserving structure.

Architectural Thermal Venting

Ventilation becomes part of the form language rather than a technical afterthought.

04 — Workspace

At Home in Creative Spaces

A restrained silhouette and premium CMF direction allow the object to sit naturally in a modern workspace without becoming a visual distraction.

05 — Engineering Intent

Performance Without Visual Noise

The concept balances flagship connectivity with a simplified exterior language: concealed antennas, passive cooling, and a focused rear I/O layout.
